Staying Ahead: How to Spot Trending Tech for IT Engineers

Posted by Saravanesh on October 10, 2024

In the past, mastering languages like Java or .NET or some SQL tools was enough for a solid career. But today, companies want more—engineers who can code and work with cloud platforms, deployment tools, automation, data analytics, Machine learning and the lists go on.

The Shift from Language-Centric to Ecosystem-Centric Learning

Back then, being a “Java Developer” was the goal. Now, it’s about the ecosystem—companies need engineers who can deploy apps on AWS, manage Kubernetes clusters, and automate infrastructure. I’ve been through this change myself. A few years ago, I made the switch from Mainframe to AWS. I saw firsthand how important it is to not only adapt but to continuously evolve with the industry.

What’s even more surprising is that this shift isn’t just for people with IT backgrounds. I’ve seen a 20-year chemistry teacher and a 7-year civil engineer transition into AWS DevOps roles through learning the right tools and skills. The opportunities are there—you just need to know how to align your learning with the market.

Steps to Stay Updated:

  • Browse LinkedIn Job Section: Search for roles in your field and notice the tools and skills listed beyond programming languages. Job descriptions reflect what the industry actually needs right now.
  • Spot Recurring Skills: Identify patterns—if companies are asking for Java with Kubernetes or .NET with Azure, that’s your signal to start learning those skills.
  • Understand Role Descriptions: Read beyond the surface. Companies now need engineers who can combine coding with cloud and DevOps skills. Pay attention to how skills are paired and why.
  • Align Learning with Demand: Don’t just stick to languages. Learn the broader ecosystem—CI/CD, cloud platforms, containers. This alignment will make you a more versatile candidate.
  • Track the Market: Follow tech blogs, industry reports, and job postings. Trends shift quickly, so knowing what’s in demand keeps your learning relevant.

The Importance of Adaptability

The IT industry now demands more than just coding—you need to understand how your code fits into a broader, cloud-native, and automated system. I’ve experienced this shift firsthand, and I’ve seen others, even from non-IT backgrounds, make the leap into tech roles. With the right learning strategy, you can adapt and thrive in this new landscape.
